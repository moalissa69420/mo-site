Regulatory & Liability

Careful Navigation Required

Critical Constraint: AI cannot legally serve as final approval authority for building plans in any US jurisdiction. Building official authority derives from state statutes requiring a licensed human professional to sign off.

Key Regulatory Facts

  • PE stamp requirements: Only licensed PEs can accept "full legal responsibility" for reviewed plans (NY Education Law §7209)
  • Honolulu pilot positioning: Explicitly avoids "automating everything" or providing "yes-or-no answers"
  • Practical implication: Must position as "decision support" not "decision maker"

Union Opposition Risk

AFSCME has documented resolutions opposing automation and privatization. Building department staff are typically union-represented in larger jurisdictions.

Mitigation: Market as "making reviewers more productive" not "replacing plan reviewers"

Liability Allocation

  • Municipalities: Substantial sovereign immunity for discretionary plan review (CA Gov Code §818.6)
  • Design professionals: Remain primarily liable regardless of AI assistance
  • Software vendors: Expect broad hold-harmless agreements and $5M-$10M professional liability minimums

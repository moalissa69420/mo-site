01.

The Infinite Palette

An immersive experience that combines collaboration with individualism to create the new standard.

Prepared By: Abdou Sarr & Mo Alissa — December 10th, 2021

02.

The Shirley Card

“Photography is not just a system of calibrating light, but a technology of subjective decisions”.

The original Shirley card ushered in an era of color correction where “Shirley” was the standard and left diverse skin tones out of the picture.

03.

Background: The evolution of standard

Since the 1990s, major strides have been made to improve how cameras see color and new standards have been put into place.

The truth however, is that in a world full of infinite unique and individual skin tones, there is no true standard.

04.

Introduction

We are so much more than a color code. The goal of this immersive art exhibit is to highlight the idea that the new standard is to not conform to a standard at all.

The Infinite Palette encourages attendees to form a new color palette (a human standard) in real time—visualizing the infinite range of our unique skin tones.

This palette is made from their skin tones and names (how they see the canvas digitally), instead of generic tones and color codes (how they see the canvas physically).

05.

The Capture Experience

Attendees see a large color‑grid canvas on the wall with a booth in front. Using a Pixel device app, they capture a selfie and add their name. Once submitted, their image is sent to the digital art piece.

Optional: a sub‑experience shows what their photo would have looked like if corrected with the original Shirley card.

06.

The AR Experience — Viewing the Digital Palette

In real life, the canvas is just a grid of colors (representing the old standard). The viewing app brings the canvas to life via AR.

As more submissions arrive, viewers see the message forming in real time—subtle nuances and variations of each individual’s skin tone coming together into an infinite human color palette.

In the app, viewers see a grid of captured people, tones as backgrounds, and names below.

07.

The Purpose — Our Objective

  • Educate: understand past & present via the non‑digital experience.
  • Absorb: personal capture + submission app.
  • Immerse: interactive AR evolves throughout the day.
  • Embody: leave with a new perspective on color identity.
